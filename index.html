Accounts and Cards

Accounts

As we have said, bank accounts are a financial product, where its main function is a place to deposit physical money for its movement and administration. There are many types of accounts, the most important of which are savings accounts and current accounts. The peculiarities of bank accounts are the following: They usually have a commission: in most cases you will have to pay a monthly commission for the maintenance and administration of the bank account. There are different forms of payment: the accounts allow you to have associated payments, such as cheque books, debit and credit cards. There is instant liquidity: when you have the account, you will have access to your money, if you want it, either at the moment, by transfer, cash machine, etc. Each bank account has a code that identifies each one of them. There are several types of accounts, such as current, savings, salary, remunerated, for young people, etc.

Cards

A bank card is a means of payment issued by a financial institution to a consumer, through an associated account, which is accepted in many shops and establishments. The purpose of a bank card is to be able to make cash withdrawals from ATMs or buy any type of goods and services, both in physical shops and online. There are many types of bank cards on the market. Below, we will describe their types and main characteristics:

  • Debit card
  • Credit card

Mortgages

The mortgage is a real right of guarantee, which is constituted to ensure the fulfilment of an obligation (normally the payment of a credit or loan), which confers on its holder a right of realisation of the value of an asset, (generally real estate) which, although encumbered, remains in the possession of its owner, and the mortgagee may, in the event that the secured debt is not satisfied within the agreed period, promote the forced sale of the asset encumbered with the Mortgage, whoever the holder is at that time, in the event that the secured debt is not satisfied within the agreed period, to promote the forced sale of the property encumbered with the Mortgage, whoever the holder may be at that time (reipersecutoriedad) in order to, with the amount thereof, make payment of the credit owed, up to the amount obtained from the forced sale promoted for the realisation of the Mortgaged property.

Loans

It is nothing more than an operation by which a financial entity makes available to a client or company a certain amount of money that it will have the obligation to repay - together with interest - in a number of instalments previously established by contract. Therefore, a bank loan is a commitment acquired by both parties (entity and contractor) and it is important to know the elements that make it up: Capital: amount of money requested from the financial entity Interest: amount that the customer or company pays to the entity for having the borrowed capital Term: the number of periodic instalments fixed in the contract to return the borrowed money plus interest. The reasons for requesting a bank loan can be diverse, although they are often related to the purchase of a specific good or service.
